# CFO Playbook — Red Flag Checklists

Quick-reference red flag checklists by domain. Use these as a final sweep when reviewing any deal document.

---

## Cashflow Quality

- [ ] EBITDA growing but MFCF flat or declining (cash quality eroding)
- [ ] EBITDA-to-MFCF conversion below 50% without clear structural explanation
- [ ] Persistent positive WC adjustments in EBITDA bridges (WC consuming cash, not generating)
- [ ] Total capex close to D&A with no maintenance/growth split disclosed
- [ ] Management classifies 80%+ of capex as "growth" — cherry-picking the label
- [ ] SBC large and consistently added back in "adjusted" metrics
- [ ] MFCF/Net Assets ratio declining despite EBITDA growth
- [ ] FCF only positive in H2 but presented as structural
- [ ] Significant gap between accounting D&A and actual capex

---

## Working Capital

- [ ] Positive NWC in a business that should structurally be negative (SaaS, retail)
- [ ] DSO trending up without revenue model explanation
- [ ] DPO declining — losing supplier negotiating leverage
- [ ] Inventory DIO spiking — demand slowdown or buildup
- [ ] WC % of revenue worsening over 3+ years
- [ ] WC improved sharply in the quarter immediately before transaction
- [ ] High AR concentration (top 3 customers >40% of AR)
- [ ] Deferred revenue declining in a subscription business
- [ ] WC "adjustments" consistently additive to EBITDA in management reporting
- [ ] 90+ day AR bucket above 10% of total AR

---

## CapEx Evaluation

- [ ] Reported maintenance capex consistently below D&A (deferred maintenance cliff)
- [ ] 80%+ of total capex labeled "growth" by management
- [ ] No stage-gate structure on speculative projects
- [ ] Every business case shows positive NPV or IRR above hurdle (hurdle rate not real)
- [ ] Projects regularly 25%+ over budget or timeline
- [ ] No PIR culture — no accountability loop
- [ ] Growth capex deployed without matching EBITDA improvement (Nathan problem)
- [ ] CapEx envelope sized by available cash, not opportunity quality
- [ ] SaaS/tech company with no framework applied to R&D or S&M spend

---

## Capital Allocation

- [ ] EBITDA growing but MFCF per share flat or declining
- [ ] Strategy and capital flows don't align
- [ ] Latent capital with no stated deployment plan
- [ ] Dividends paid while balance sheet is impaired or covenants are tight
- [ ] Opex growing faster than revenue with no articulated return metric
- [ ] M&A multiples consistently higher than organic return opportunities
- [ ] No written financial policy
- [ ] "Every project is NPV positive" — hurdle rates aren't real
- [ ] SBC dilution growing faster than MFCF per share improvement
- [ ] Growth capex deployed without matching EBITDA improvement

---

## Deal Diligence

- [ ] EBITDA adjustment count > 8 lines
- [ ] A "one-off" item appears in multiple periods
- [ ] Management resistant to providing monthly P&L or WC schedule
- [ ] Audited accounts differ materially from management accounts
- [ ] Revenue recognition policy more aggressive than industry peers
- [ ] SG&A suspiciously low vs revenue in LTM (headcount gap, cost cuts pre-sale)
- [ ] Capex materially below D&A for 2+ years
- [ ] Tax returns not provided or significantly redacted
- [ ] Key customer contract not in data room
- [ ] Change-of-control provisions not disclosed
- [ ] WC improved sharply in the final reporting period before signing

---

## Deal Structuring

- [ ] Cash-only demand from seller on a business where buyer is taking significant risk
- [ ] No seller note despite significant information asymmetry
- [ ] No offset rights against seller note (lose protection on post-close claims)
- [ ] Earnout duration > 3 years
- [ ] EBITDA earnout with no cost allocation restrictions (buyer can kill it)
- [ ] Seller not remaining post-close in earnout structure
- [ ] WC peg left to documentation stage (value left on the table)
- [ ] Shares deal with weak reps and warranties and no R&W insurance
- [ ] SPA limitation period < 18 months for complex tax/legal exposures
- [ ] Headline price agreed before terms reviewed

---

## Value Creation

- [ ] Value creation plan has no owner for specific pillars
- [ ] Growth investment with no stage-gate or return metric
- [ ] Nathan problem: capex/acquisition spend without EBITDA improvement
- [ ] Playing Lego without an integration playbook
- [ ] Lemon squeezed too hard — SG&A/R&D below sustainable run-rate
- [ ] Polish the Trophy being attempted without EBITDA substance
- [ ] No synergy tracking — synergies "in the model" but unowned
- [ ] No skeleton IM 12–18 months before exit
- [ ] Exit thesis depends entirely on multiple expansion in a rising market
- [ ] Management team assessed as inadequate for PE pace/intensity

---

## Capital Structure

- [ ] DSCR below 1.2x at base case
- [ ] Leverage above industry-appropriate range at current EBITDA
- [ ] Debt maturity wall within 18 months with underperforming EBITDA
- [ ] Covenants with <15% headroom under base case
- [ ] Capex covenant limiting maintenance spend below sustainable level
- [ ] No RCF or liquidity facility — zero buffer
- [ ] PIK interest accruing on significant portion of debt
- [ ] Capital structure sized at peak-cycle EBITDA
- [ ] Lender is a distressed/special situations fund (implies prior desperation)
- [ ] Minimum liquidity covenant tested weekly at a level that creates operational friction

---

## Cross-Cutting / Common Pitfalls

- [ ] Nathan problem confirmed across the portfolio
- [ ] Strategy–capital disconnect confirmed
- [ ] No history of rejected projects (hurdle rates not real)
- [ ] WACC being used to justify inadequate returns on PE-backed business
- [ ] Key person risk not contractually mitigated
- [ ] Integration capability inadequate for the buy-and-build pace
- [ ] LTM EBITDA heavily adjusted (>8 lines)
- [ ] Anchoring to management projections (no independent build)
- [ ] Sunk cost fallacy active in DD continuation decision
- [ ] Confirmation bias — no adversarial DD on key assumptions
- [ ] Winner's curse risk — won competitive process without clear information advantage
- [ ] Exit thesis depends entirely on factors outside our control
