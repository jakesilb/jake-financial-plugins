# CFO Playbook — Metrics Glossary

Key metrics and terms used across all CFO playbook skills.

---

## Cashflow Metrics

**MFCF (Maintainable Free Cashflow)**
Cash generated from existing operations — the CFO's primary measure of business health. Calculated as: EBITDA - SBC - Maintenance CapEx - Maintenance R&D - WC movements - Interest - Tax. Separates cash generation (above the line) from capital allocation decisions (below the line).

**MFCF Return on Net Assets**
MFCF ÷ Net Assets (PP&E + NWC + Intangibles). Measures capital efficiency. Should improve over time as existing assets are sweated harder. Strong LTIP metric.

**MFCF per Share**
MFCF ÷ Diluted share count. The north star metric — maximizing this is the CFO's first duty. Captures both cash generation quality and dilution discipline.

**CFADS (Cashflow Available for Debt Service)**
EBITDA - Cash Tax - Maintenance CapEx - WC movements - Mandatory amortization. Used to assess true debt capacity. More accurate than EBITDA multiples for leverage assessment.

**DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)**
CFADS ÷ Total Debt Service (principal + interest). Minimum typically 1.1–1.3x for lenders. Conservative target: 1.5x+.

**CAG (Cashflow Accountability Grid)**
Management reporting tool: allocates accountability for each MFCF component to one named individual. Rows = MFCF components; Columns = business units/divisions.

**CTG (Cashflow Target Grid)**
Budget version of the CAG — sets MFCF targets by component × division. Foundation for the cashflow culture.

**CPG (Cashflow Performance Grid)**
Actual vs CTG variance report. Reported monthly + YTD. The primary cashflow performance management tool.

**CCC (Cash Conversion Cycle)**
DIO + DSO - DPO. Negative CCC = business is self-funding (gets paid before it pays). Positive CCC = business is cash-consuming as it grows.

## Working Capital Metrics

**NWC (Net Working Capital)**
Trade Receivables + Inventory + Cash Float + Prepayments (operating) - Trade Payables - Accrued Liabilities - Deferred Revenue. Operating NWC excludes cash balances and financing items.

**Cash Float**
Minimum cash needed to operate (e.g., till floats, petty cash, minimum bank balances). Part of NWC — not available for capital allocation. Distinguished from total cash balance.

**DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)**
(Trade Receivables ÷ Revenue) × 365. How long customers take to pay.

**DPO (Days Payables Outstanding)**
(Trade Payables ÷ COGS or Purchases) × 365. How long the company takes to pay suppliers.

**DIO (Days Inventory Outstanding)**
(Inventory ÷ COGS) × 365. How long inventory sits before being sold.

**WC Peg**
In M&A: the agreed NWC level the seller delivers at closing. If actual NWC > peg, buyer pays seller; if actual NWC < peg, seller refunds buyer. Worth 1–3% of EV in negotiation.

## CapEx Metrics

**Maintenance CapEx**
Recurring spend required to maintain current operating capacity. If not spent, revenue declines. The "coffee machine test": if stopping the spend causes revenue to fall, it's maintenance.

**Growth CapEx**
Spend that creates new capacity or increases future cash generation. Excluded from MFCF — it's a capital allocation decision.

**CROI (Cash Return on Investment)**
Mature annual cash generation ÷ total upfront investment. Simple, easy to communicate. Ignores time value and the J-curve — useful but not primary.

**NPV per $ Invested**
NPV ÷ total capex invested. Captures both time value of money and capital efficiency. Preferred secondary metric alongside payback period.

**Payback Period**
Time to recover the upfront investment from cashflows. Simple, universally understood, strong for downside protection. Preferred primary metric.

**Hard Return**
CapEx with proven payback — automation, cost removal, proven rollout programs. Tight outcome distribution.

**Soft Return**
CapEx with speculative payback — new markets, unproven demand, untested payback. Wide, fat-tailed outcome distribution.

**CapEx Envelope**
The total capital budget decided before evaluating individual projects. Sized by: access to capital, cost of capital, quality of pipeline, execution ability.

## Capital Allocation

**Financial Policy**
The written rulebook governing how a business funds itself and treats shareholders. Sets leverage targets, dividend policy, return hurdle rates, M&A criteria. Without it, decisions are ad hoc.

**ZBB (Zero-Based Budgeting)**
Justify every cost from zero rather than incrementing last year's base. Applied to discretionary growth opex — treat each dollar as a capex decision requiring a return.

**Stage-Gate**
Process for committing capital to speculative projects in tranches: Gate 1 (exploratory) → Gate 2 (pilot) → Gate 3 (full rollout). Capital committed at each gate proportional to confidence in return.

## Deal Metrics

**EV (Enterprise Value)**
Total value of the business: Market Cap + Net Debt (or: EBITDA × multiple for private companies).

**Equity Value**
EV - Net Debt. What equity holders receive.

**MOIC (Multiple on Invested Capital)**
Exit Equity Value ÷ Equity Invested. How many times you made your money back.

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return)**
Annualized return on investment. Sensitive to hold period and interim cashflows. Can be gamed via reinvestment rate assumptions — not primary metric.

**EV/EBITDA Multiple**
Enterprise value divided by EBITDA. Primary valuation currency in PE. Entry vs exit multiple spread is a key return driver.

**QoE (Quality of Earnings)**
Assessment of whether reported EBITDA represents true, sustainable, run-rate earnings. The most important limb of financial DD.

**LTM (Last Twelve Months)**
Rolling 12-month period — the standard basis for M&A EBITDA measurement.

**NTM (Next Twelve Months)**
Forward 12 months — used in growth deals where forward earnings justify the price.

**Adjustment Line Count**
Number of EBITDA bridge adjustments between reported and adjusted EBITDA. Signal of earnings quality: 1–3 = clean, 8+ = red flag.

## Capital Structure Metrics

**Leverage Ratio**
Net Debt ÷ EBITDA. The primary leverage metric in PE. Typically covenanted at a maximum level.

**Net Debt**
Gross debt - cash (excluding minimum operating cash float).

**TLA/TLB (Term Loan A/B)**
TLA: amortizing, typically bank-held. TLB: minimal amortization (1%/year), institutional-held, bullet maturity. TLB is the standard PE debt instrument.

**RCF (Revolving Credit Facility)**
Drawn/repaid as needed; provides liquidity buffer. Critical for WC-intensive businesses.

**PIK (Payment in Kind)**
Interest that accrues to principal rather than being paid in cash. Preserves CFADS but compounds rapidly — use sparingly.

**Unitranche**
Single lender providing all debt (combines senior + junior in one instrument). Simpler, faster, higher cost. Common in PE mid-market.

**Capital Desperation Trap**
Situation where a business underperforms and lacks CFADS to refinance on market terms. Forced into distressed/vulture lending at punishing rates. Avoided by sizing structure conservatively and maintaining covenant headroom.

## Value Creation

**7 Pillars**
The PE CFO value creation framework:
1. Traveling Light — capital efficiency
2. Playing Lego — buy-and-build
3. Fillet the Fish — perimeter shifting / disposals
4. Squeezing the Lemon — efficiency / margin expansion
5. Open the Throttle — growth enablement
6. Stop the Bleed — turnaround
7. Polish the Trophy — exit preparation / multiple expansion

**PIR (Post-Investment Review)**
Formal assessment of whether a capex project delivered the promised returns. Critical accountability mechanism — without it, there's no incentive to forecast honestly.

**Skeleton IM**
Draft Information Memorandum built 12–18 months before exit to rehearse the exit narrative, identify weaknesses, and allow time to fix them before buyer DD.
